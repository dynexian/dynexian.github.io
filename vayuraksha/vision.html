AirAlgae Research Program and Long-Term Direction

This page holds the scientific narrative behind Vayu Raksha: what we are validating now, how the AirAlgae team is translating biology into reliable hardware, and how each execution phase maps to measurable outcomes.

Deep-Dive Research

The hybrid system combines mechanical particulate capture with a biologically active chamber. Our active study tracks particulate reduction, volatile organic compound conversion behavior, algae vitality under urban load, and safe maintenance intervals for long-running deployments.

Filtration science

Modeling particle residence time through staged airflow paths to identify minimum dwell time for effective capture and conversion.

Bioreactor behavior

Monitoring growth curves, pH drift, and nutrient cycling to maintain algae health while preserving predictable purification output.

Sensor validation

Cross-checking PM2.5, VOC, and humidity streams with calibrated reference instruments to maintain confidence in user-facing data.

Lifecycle and safety

Studying cleaning windows, contamination risk, and fail-safe shutdown conditions for household-safe operation.

AirAlgae Team Vision

The long-term ambition is to move from passive filtering devices to adaptive indoor ecosystems. AirAlgae is building toward a modular respiratory platform where each unit learns local air patterns, tunes conversion behavior, and contributes to shared city-scale environmental intelligence.

1 to 2 year horizon

Reliable household units with transparent metrics, repeatable maintenance, and strong user trust in measured outcomes.

3 to 5 year horizon

Networked systems for schools and offices with predictive alerts and centralized diagnostics.
